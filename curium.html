Curium (Cm)

Atomic Number: 96

Basic Information

Symbol: Cm
Atomic Mass: [247] u (most stable isotope)
Category: Actinide
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Rn] 5f⁷ 6d¹ 7s²

Key Properties

  • Silvery-white, dense radioactive metal, glows purple in the dark
  • Named after Marie and Pierre Curie, pioneers of radioactivity
  • Intense alpha emitter — 1 gram of ²⁴⁴Cm produces ~3 watts of heat
  • Paramagnetic, with unusual magnetic ordering at low temperatures
  • First element ever produced in visible quantities (1940s)

Discovery & History

Discovered in 1944 by Glenn T. Seaborg, Ralph A. James, and Albert Ghiorso at the University of California, Berkeley. They bombarded plutonium-239 with alpha particles in the 60-inch cyclotron, producing curium-242.

Named in honor of Marie and Pierre Curie for their contributions to the understanding of radioactivity. The discovery was kept secret until after World War II.

Historical Timeline:
  • 1944: First synthesized at Berkeley (²⁴²Cm)
  • 1947: First isolation of a curium compound (Cm(OH)₃) by Werner and Perlman
  • 1951: First curium metal produced
  • Modern: Used in space probes and as alpha particle source

Common Uses

Radioisotope thermoelectric generators (space probes)
Alpha particle source for X-ray spectrometers
Power source for lunar and planetary missions
Production of heavier transuranium elements
Scientific research in nuclear physics
Cancer therapy research (targeted alpha therapy)
Calibration sources for radiation detectors
Component of some pacemaker batteries (historical)

Interesting Facts

  • Curium is the only element named after a married couple (Marie and Pierre Curie).
  • It glows a brilliant purple in the dark due to its intense alpha radiation exciting surrounding air molecules.
  • ²⁴²Cm has a half-life of only 162 days, producing tremendous heat — a gram generates about 120 watts.
  • Curium(III) ions exhibit strong fluorescence, glowing orange-red under UV light.
  • It's so radioactive that it self-destructs its crystal lattice over time (metamictization).
  • Total world production is only a few grams per year, primarily for scientific research.

Atomic Structure

96 protons, 151 neutrons (in 247Cm), 96 electrons

Try These Curium Reactions

Curium chemistry is dominated by the +3 oxidation state, similar to its lanthanide analogue gadolinium. The +4 state can be achieved under strongly oxidizing conditions but is less stable.

Experiment Idea: In solution, Cm³⁺ ions are pale yellow or greenish and exhibit strong orange-red fluorescence under UV light — a distinctive feature. Curium dioxide (CmO₂) can be prepared by heating the metal or oxalate in oxygen. Due to the intense heat and radioactivity (especially from ²⁴²Cm and ²⁴⁴Cm), all work requires remote handling in specialized hot cells.
