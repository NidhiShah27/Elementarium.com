Helium (He)

Atomic Number: 2

Basic Information

Symbol: He
Atomic Mass: 4.002602 u
Category: Noble Gas
State at Room Temperature: Gas
Electron Configuration:
1s²

Key Properties

  • Colorless, odorless, tasteless noble gas
  • Second lightest element (after hydrogen)
  • Lowest boiling point of any element (-268.9°C)
  • Completely inert – forms no compounds
  • Second most abundant element in the universe

Discovery & History

First detected in 1868 by Pierre Janssen during a solar eclipse. Norman Lockyer and Edward Frankland named it after the Greek sun god, Helios.

First discovered on Earth in 1895 by Sir William Ramsay in cleveite ore.

Historical Timeline:
  • 1868: Janssen and Lockyer discover helium in the Sun's spectrum
  • 1895: Ramsay isolates helium on Earth
  • 1903: Helium discovered in natural gas wells
  • 20th c.: Used in airships, balloons, and cryogenics

Common Uses

Party balloons and airships
Cryogenic cooling (MRI magnets)
Helium-neon lasers
Pressurizing rocket fuel tanks
Welding (shielding gas)
Gas chromatography carrier gas
Superconducting magnets coolant
Deep-sea diving breathing mixtures

Interesting Facts

  • Helium is the only element discovered first in space (the Sun) before being found on Earth
  • Liquid helium can flow uphill and through microscopic cracks due to superfluidity
  • Helium is non-renewable on Earth – once released into the atmosphere, it escapes into space forever
  • Helium remains liquid down to absolute zero – it never freezes at normal pressure
  • Helium makes up about 24% of the universe's elemental mass (hydrogen is 75%)
  • Inhaling helium temporarily changes your voice – it's lighter than air, so sound travels faster

Atomic Structure

2 protons, 2 neutrons, 2 electrons
