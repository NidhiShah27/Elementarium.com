Silicon (Si)

Atomic Number: 14

Basic Information

Symbol: Si
Atomic Mass: 28.0855 u
Category: Metalloid
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Ne] 3s² 3p²

Key Properties

  • Hard, crystalline metalloid with blue-grey luster
  • Second most abundant element in Earth's crust
  • Semiconducting properties (foundation of electronics)
  • High melting point (1414°C)
  • Forms a protective oxide layer (SiO₂)

Discovery & History

First isolated by Jöns Jacob Berzelius in 1824 in Sweden. He heated potassium fluorosilicate with potassium to produce amorphous silicon.

The name comes from Latin "silex" or "silicis" meaning flint.

Historical Timeline:
  • 1787: Lavoisier suspects silicon is an element
  • 1824: Berzelius isolates pure silicon
  • 1854: Crystalline silicon produced
  • 1940s: Silicon semiconductors revolutionize electronics

Common Uses

Computer chips and transistors
Solar panels
Glass and ceramics
Concrete and cement
Smartphone components
Medical implants
Silicones and lubricants
Gemstones (opal, agate)

Interesting Facts

  • Silicon Valley is named after this element – the heart of the tech industry
  • Silicon makes up 27.7% of Earth's crust – second only to oxygen
  • Silicates (silicon-oxygen compounds) form most rocks and minerals
  • Some plants (like horsetails) accumulate silica for structural strength
  • Pure silicon is rare in nature – it's almost always bound with oxygen
  • The first working transistor (1947) was made from germanium, but silicon soon took over

Atomic Structure

14 protons, 14 neutrons, 14 electrons
