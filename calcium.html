Calcium (Ca)

Atomic Number: 20

Basic Information

Symbol: Ca
Atomic Mass: 40.078 u
Category: Alkaline Earth Metal
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Ar] 4s²

Key Properties

  • Essential for bones and teeth (as calcium phosphate)
  • Burns with a brick-red flame
  • Reacts with water to produce hydrogen
  • Relatively soft and silvery metal
  • Fifth most abundant element in Earth's crust

Discovery & History

First isolated by Humphry Davy in 1808 in England through electrolysis of a mixture of lime (calcium oxide) and mercury oxide.

The name comes from Latin "calx" meaning lime, as calcium compounds have been used since ancient times for making plaster and cement.

Historical Timeline:
  • Ancient: Lime used for mortar and plaster
  • 1808: Davy isolates calcium metal
  • 19th c.: Understanding of calcium in bones
  • 20th c.: Essential nutrient research

Common Uses

Calcium supplements (bone health)
Cement and concrete
Chalk and plaster (gypsum)
Reducing agent in metallurgy
Lead-acid batteries (calcium alloy)
Food preservative
Toothpaste and dental products
Fertilizer additive

Interesting Facts

  • An adult human body contains about 1 kg of calcium – 99% of it is in bones and teeth
  • Calcium compounds produce a brick-red flame, used in fireworks and flares
  • Calcium reacts with water to form calcium hydroxide and hydrogen – less vigorous than sodium, but still energetic
  • Calcium is the fifth most abundant element in Earth's crust (about 4%)
  • Stalactites and stalagmites in caves are made of calcium carbonate (CaCO₃)
  • Eggshells are about 94% calcium carbonate – the same mineral as chalk and limestone

Atomic Structure

20 protons, 20 neutrons, 20 electrons
