Oxygen (O)

Atomic Number: 8

Basic Information

Symbol: O
Atomic Mass: 15.999 u
Category: Nonmetal
State at Room Temperature: Gas
Electron Configuration:
[He] 2s² 2p⁴

Key Properties

  • Colorless, odorless, tasteless gas
  • Supports combustion (but does not burn)
  • Essential for respiration in most living organisms
  • Makes up 21% of Earth's atmosphere
  • Slightly soluble in water (essential for aquatic life)

Discovery & History

Discovered independently by Carl Wilhelm Scheele (1771) in Sweden and Joseph Priestley (1774) in England. Antoine Lavoisier named it "oxygen" in 1777.

The name comes from Greek "oxys" (acid) and "genes" (forming) – Lavoisier mistakenly thought it was a component of all acids.

Historical Timeline:
  • 1771: Scheele produces oxygen ("fire air")
  • 1774: Priestley independently discovers oxygen
  • 1777: Lavoisier names it oxygen
  • 1885: Liquid oxygen first produced

Common Uses

Medical oxygen (respiratory therapy)
Steel production (basic oxygen process)
Rocket propellant (liquid oxygen)
Welding and cutting (oxy-acetylene)
Water treatment (ozone, O₃)
Chemical oxidation reactions
Bleaching in paper industry
Wastewater treatment aeration

Interesting Facts

  • Oxygen is the third most abundant element in the universe (by mass)
  • Makes up 89% of water by mass and 21% of air by volume
  • Oxygen is the most abundant element in Earth's crust (46% by mass)
  • The ozone layer (O₃) protects Earth from harmful UV radiation
  • Liquid oxygen is pale blue and magnetic
  • Nearly all oxygen in the atmosphere is produced by photosynthesis

Atomic Structure

8 protons, 8 neutrons, 8 electrons
