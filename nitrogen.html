Nitrogen (N)

Atomic Number: 7

Basic Information

Symbol: N
Atomic Mass: 14.007 u
Category: Nonmetal
State at Room Temperature: Gas
Electron Configuration:
[He] 2s² 2p³

Key Properties

  • Colorless, odorless, tasteless gas
  • Boils at -195.8°C (liquid nitrogen)
  • Makes up 78% of Earth's atmosphere
  • Relatively inert under normal conditions
  • Essential component of all living tissue (proteins, DNA)

Discovery & History

Discovered by Daniel Rutherford in 1772, who called it "noxious air." Antoine Lavoisier named it "azote" meaning "without life."

The name "nitrogen" comes from Greek "nitron" (native soda) and "genes" (forming) – referring to its presence in nitre (saltpeter).

Historical Timeline:
  • 1772: Rutherford isolates nitrogen
  • 1790: Lavoisier names it "azote"
  • 1908: Liquid nitrogen first produced
  • 1913: Haber-Bosch process developed for ammonia

Common Uses

Liquid nitrogen coolant
Ammonia production (fertilizer)
Food preservation (inert atmosphere)
Stainless steel production
Tire filling (inert, maintains pressure)
Fire suppression systems
Cryopreservation (sperm, eggs, embryos)
Explosives manufacturing (TNT, nitroglycerin)

Interesting Facts

  • Air is 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and 1% other gases
  • Nitrogen is a key component of amino acids and DNA
  • Lightning fixes nitrogen – it converts N₂ into nitrogen oxides that fertilize soil
  • The Haber-Bosch process (making ammonia) feeds half the world's population
  • Liquid nitrogen is used in molecular gastronomy to create instant ice cream
  • Liquid nitrogen expands 700 times when it vaporizes

Atomic Structure

7 protons, 7 neutrons, 7 electrons
