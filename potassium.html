Potassium (K)

Atomic Number: 19

Basic Information

Symbol: K
Atomic Mass: 39.0983 u
Category: Alkali Metal
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Ar] 4s¹

Key Properties

  • Extremely reactive with water – ignites spontaneously
  • Soft, silvery metal (cut with a knife)
  • Low density – floats on water (but reacts violently!)
  • Essential for nerve function in living organisms
  • Seventh most abundant element in Earth's crust

Discovery & History

First isolated by Humphry Davy in 1807 in England through electrolysis of molten potassium hydroxide (caustic potash).

The name comes from the English word "potash" (pot ashes) and the Latin "kalium".

Historical Timeline:
  • 1807: Davy isolates potassium
  • 1860s: Potassium fertilizers developed
  • 20th c.: Essential for plant nutrition
  • Today: Vital for agriculture and medicine

Common Uses

Fertilizers (potash)
Food preservatives (KCl salt substitute)
Laboratory reagent
Soap manufacturing
Glass and ceramics
Fire extinguishers
Potassium iodide (radiation pills)
Potassium-ion batteries (emerging)

Interesting Facts

  • Potassium reacts even more violently with water than sodium – it ignites instantly and burns with a lilac flame
  • Bananas are famous for their potassium content – a medium banana contains about 422 mg
  • Potassium ions are essential for nerve impulses, muscle contractions, and heart function
  • Potassium-40 in the human body undergoes radioactive decay – about 4,000 atoms decay every second inside you!
  • Potassium is one of the three essential plant nutrients (NPK: Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium)
  • Despite being a metal, potassium is so soft you can easily cut it with a plastic knife

Atomic Structure

19 protons, 20 neutrons, 19 electrons
