Francium (Fr)

Atomic Number: 87

Basic Information

Symbol: Fr
Atomic Mass: [223] u (most stable isotope)
Category: Alkali Metal
State at Room Temperature: Liquid (predicted)
Electron Configuration:
[Rn] 7s¹

Key Properties

  • Extremely radioactive – all isotopes decay rapidly
  • Most reactive of all alkali metals (predicted)
  • Second rarest naturally occurring element
  • Predicted to melt just above room temperature (~27°C)
  • Less than 30 grams present in Earth's crust at any time

Discovery & History

Discovered in 1939 by Marguerite Perey at the Curie Institute in Paris, France. She was studying actinium-227 and detected a new decay product.

Named after France, Perey's homeland. It was the last element discovered in nature (not synthetic).

Historical Timeline:
  • 1939: Perey discovers francium
  • 1946: Officially named francium
  • 1970s: Last chemical studies (trace amounts)
  • Today: Only studied through theory and decay products

Interesting Facts

  • Francium is so rare that it's estimated there's only about 20-30 grams in the entire Earth's crust at any given time
  • It's the most unstable of the first 101 elements – its most stable isotope, Fr-223, has a half-life of just 22 minutes
  • Francium would be the most reactive metal – if you could collect enough, it would explode on contact with water
  • It's predicted to melt at about 27°C – it would be liquid in a warm room
  • Marguerite Perey was the first and only woman to discover an element (excluding those discovered in ancient times)
  • No one has ever seen a visible piece of francium – all studies are done with trace amounts too small to see

Atomic Structure

87 protons, 136 neutrons (in 223Fr), 87 electrons
