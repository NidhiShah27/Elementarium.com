Alkaline Earth Metals

Group 2 Elements

Group: 2 (IIA)

Category: Alkaline Earth Metal

Elements: Beryllium (Be), Magnesium (Mg), Calcium (Ca), Strontium (Sr), Barium (Ba), Radium (Ra)

General Electron Configuration: ns2

Alkaline earth metals are shiny, silvery-white metals that are somewhat reactive at standard temperature and pressure. They are harder and denser than alkali metals.

They react with water (except Beryllium) to produce alkaline solutions and hydrogen gas.

Key Properties:
  • All have two valence electrons which they lose to form +2 cations.
  • Reactivity increases down the group (Barium is more reactive than Calcium).
  • They have higher melting points than alkali metals.
  • Good conductors of electricity but less so than alkali metals.
  • They produce characteristic flame colors: Ca (brick red), Sr (crimson red), Ba (apple green).
  • React with oxygen to form oxides (MO) and with water to form hydroxides (M(OH)₂).
  • Essential for biological functions: Calcium for bones/teeth, Magnesium for enzymes.
Common Uses:
  • Beryllium: Aerospace components, X-ray windows, alloys for springs.
  • Magnesium: Lightweight alloys, fireworks (white light), antacids.
  • Calcium: Construction materials (cement, plaster), dietary supplements.
  • Strontium: Fireworks (red color), glass for color TVs, toothpaste for sensitive teeth.
  • Barium: Medical imaging (barium swallow), fireworks (green color), glassmaking.
  • Radium: Historical use in luminescent paints, now limited due to radioactivity.

Alkaline Earth Metals (Be, Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba samples)

Reaction with Water

Most alkaline earth metals react with water, though reactivity varies:

M(s) + 2H2O(l) → M(OH)2(aq) + H2(g)

  • Beryllium: No reaction (forms protective oxide layer)
  • Magnesium: Reacts slowly with hot water
  • Calcium: Reacts steadily with cold water
  • Strontium & Barium: React vigorously with water
