Germanium (Ge)

Atomic Number: 32

Basic Information

Symbol: Ge
Atomic Mass: 72.630 u
Category: Metalloid
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s² 4p²

Key Properties

  • Grayish-white, lustrous metalloid
  • Important semiconductor (first transistor material)
  • Transparent to infrared radiation
  • Melting point: 938°C, relatively low for a metal
  • Predicted by Mendeleev as "eka-silicon"

Discovery & History

Discovered in 1886 by Clemens Winkler in Germany. He isolated it from the mineral argyrodite and named it after his homeland.

Its existence and properties were predicted by Mendeleev in 1871 as "eka-silicon" – a major triumph of the periodic table.

Historical Timeline:
  • 1871: Mendeleev predicts eka-silicon
  • 1886: Winkler discovers germanium
  • 1947: First transistor made with germanium
  • 1950s: Silicon largely replaces it in electronics

Common Uses

Fiber optics (doping agent)
Infrared optics (lenses, windows)
Solar cells (space applications)
High-speed electronics
Catalyst in polymerization
Jewelry (germanium is hypoallergenic)
Dietary supplements (controversial)
X-ray detectors

Interesting Facts

  • Germanium was the first element to be discovered that filled a gap predicted by Mendeleev's periodic table
  • The first working transistor (1947) was made of germanium, not silicon
  • Some plants accumulate germanium, but its biological role is unknown
  • Germanium is transparent to infrared light, making it ideal for night-vision systems
  • Pure germanium is rarely seen – it's usually alloyed or doped for specific applications
  • Despite being a semiconductor, germanium has largely been replaced by silicon, which is cheaper and works better at higher temperatures

Atomic Structure

32 protons, 41 neutrons, 32 electrons
