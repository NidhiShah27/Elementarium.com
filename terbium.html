Terbium (Tb)

Atomic Number: 65

Basic Information

Symbol: Tb
Atomic Mass: 158.92535 u
Category: Lanthanide
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Xe] 4f⁹ 6s²

Key Properties

  • Silvery-white, soft, ductile metal
  • Green phosphor in trichromatic lighting
  • High magnetostriction (Terfenol-D)
  • Named after the village Ytterby, Sweden
  • Relatively stable in air, but oxidizes slowly

Discovery & History

Discovered in 1843 by Swedish chemist Carl Gustaf Mosander. He separated "yttria" into three fractions: yttria, erbia, and terbia.

Named after Ytterby, the Swedish village where the mineral gadolinite was found – the same source for yttrium, erbium, and ytterbium.

Historical Timeline:
  • 1843: Mosander isolates terbium from yttria
  • 1905: Georges Urbain purifies terbium
  • 1960s: Terbium phosphors enable color TV
  • Modern: Essential for LEDs, magnets, and sonar

Common Uses

Green phosphor in fluorescent lamps
Color TV and display phosphors
Terfenol-D (magnetostrictive alloy)
Sonar and naval sonar systems
Solid-state laser dopant
Magneto-optical recording media
Fuel cell stabilizer (ZrO₂:Tb)
Fluorescent probes in biochemistry

Interesting Facts

  • Terbium is one of four elements named after Ytterby, Sweden (along with yttrium, erbium, and ytterbium).
  • Terfenol-D (Tb₀.₃Dy₀.₇Fe₂) is an alloy that changes shape dramatically in a magnetic field – it's used in precision actuators and underwater sonar.
  • Terbium green phosphor is essential for trichromatic lighting, providing the green component that makes "warm white" LEDs and fluorescent bulbs possible.
  • Terbium has two major isotopes: ¹⁵⁹Tb (stable, 100%) and several radioactive isotopes used in research.
  • It can exist in both +3 and +4 oxidation states; Tb⁴⁺ is a strong oxidizer.
  • Abundance in Earth's crust is about 1.2 ppm, similar to silver.

Atomic Structure

65 protons, 94 neutrons (in 159Tb), 65 electrons

Try These Terbium Reactions

Terbium reacts slowly with water and quickly with acids. Its compounds are typically pale green or colorless, with strong fluorescence.
