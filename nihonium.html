Nihonium (Nh)

Atomic Number: 113

Basic Information

Symbol: Nh
Atomic Mass: [286] u (most stable isotope)
Category: Post-Transition Metal (predicted)
State at Room Temperature: Solid (predicted)
Electron Configuration:
[Rn] 5f¹⁴ 6d¹⁰ 7s² 7p¹ (predicted)

Key Properties

  • Synthetic, highly radioactive
  • First element discovered in Asia (Japan)
  • Predicted to be a volatile metal
  • Very short half-life (seconds to minutes)
  • Only a few atoms ever created

Discovery & History

Discovered in 2004 by a team at RIKEN in Japan led by Kosuke Morita. They bombarded bismuth-209 with zinc-70 and detected three atoms of nihonium.

Named after "Nihon" (日本), one of the Japanese words for Japan. It's the first element discovered in Asia.

Historical Timeline:
  • 2004: RIKEN team produces first atoms
  • 2012: Confirmed by IUPAC
  • 2016: Officially named nihonium
  • 21st c.: Only a handful of atoms ever created

Interesting Facts

  • Nihonium is the first element discovered in an Asian country – a historic achievement for Japanese science
  • The RIKEN team produced just three atoms of nihonium after months of bombardment – each atom lived for less than a second
  • Nihonium is predicted to be a volatile metal that might be a liquid at room temperature (like mercury)
  • Relativistic effects are so strong that nihonium's electron configuration might differ from lighter elements in its group
  • It's named after Japan ("Nihon") – the only element named for a country in East Asia
  • No chemical experiments have ever been performed on nihonium – its chemistry is purely theoretical
