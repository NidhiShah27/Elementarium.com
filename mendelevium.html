Mendelevium (Md)

Atomic Number: 101

Basic Information

Symbol: Md
Atomic Mass: [258] u (most stable isotope)
Category: Actinide
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Rn] 5f¹³ 7s²

Key Properties

  • Highly radioactive synthetic metal, silvery-white appearance
  • Named after Dmitri Mendeleev, father of the periodic table
  • First element produced on a one-atom-at-a-time basis
  • Relatively stable +2 oxidation state due to 5f¹³ configuration
  • Only produced in atom quantities; chemical studies extremely limited

Discovery & History

Discovered in February 1955 by Albert Ghiorso, Bernard Harvey, Gregory Choppin, Stanley G. Thompson, and Glenn T. Seaborg at the University of California, Berkeley. They bombarded einsteinium-253 with alpha particles in the 60-inch cyclotron, producing mendelevium-256 (half-life 77 minutes).

Named after Dmitri Mendeleev, who created the periodic table and predicted undiscovered elements. This was the first element named after a Russian scientist.

Historical Timeline:
  • 1955: First synthesized at Berkeley (¹⁰¹Md)
  • 1958: First isolation of mendelevium compounds
  • 1967: First chemical studies in solution
  • Modern: Used to study relativistic effects in heavy elements

Common Uses

Basic research in nuclear physics
Study of actinide chemistry (one atom at a time)
Investigation of relativistic effects in heavy elements
No commercial applications (extremely rare)
Calibration of alpha and fission detectors
Study of spontaneous fission properties
Synthesis of heavier elements (nobelium, lawrencium)
Tracer experiments with single atoms

Interesting Facts

  • Mendelevium was the first element to be discovered one atom at a time — the famous "one-atom-at-a-time" chemistry was pioneered here.
  • ²⁵⁸Md has a half-life of 51.5 days — long enough for chemical studies, but only femtogram quantities exist.
  • The +2 oxidation state is unusually stable for Md, comparable to the alkaline earth metals, due to the 5f¹³ electron configuration (one electron beyond half-filled shell).
  • The discovery experiment used only about 1 billion atoms of einsteinium (10⁹) — invisible to the naked eye.
  • It was the first element named after a Russian scientist during the Cold War, a rare scientific collaboration.
  • Total production of mendelevium is measured in atoms per minute in accelerators worldwide.

Atomic Structure

101 protons, 157 neutrons (in 258Md), 101 electrons

Try These Mendelevium Reactions

Mendelevium chemistry is unique because both +2 and +3 oxidation states are accessible. The +2 state is surprisingly stable and can be studied by co-precipitation, even with only a few atoms.

Experiment Idea: Mendelevium's +2 state can be demonstrated by co-precipitation with BaSO₄ or SrSO₄, while Md³⁺ behaves like other trivalent actinides and is not carried. Reduction of Md³⁺ to Md²⁺ can be achieved using Sm²⁺ or Zn(Hg). Due to the extremely small quantities (atoms), experiments use tracer techniques with radioactivity detection. All work requires specialized hot cells and accelerators.
