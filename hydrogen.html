Hydrogen (H)

Atomic Number: 1

Basic Information

Symbol: H
Atomic Mass: 1.008 u
Category: Nonmetal
State at Room Temperature: Gas
Electron Configuration:
1s¹

Key Properties

  • Lightest element in the universe
  • Highly flammable gas
  • Colorless, odorless, tasteless
  • Simplest atomic structure
  • Becomes liquid at -252.87°C

Discovery & History

First recognized as a distinct element by Henry Cavendish in 1766.

Most abundant element in the universe (~75% of elemental mass).

Historical Timeline:
  • 1766: Cavendish identifies "inflammable air"
  • 1783: Lavoisier names it "hydrogen"
  • 1931: Deuterium discovered
  • 1934: Tritium discovered

Common Uses

Rocket fuel
Ammonia production
Petroleum refining
Laboratory reagent
Fuel cells
Welding gas
Balloon filling
Clean energy

Interesting Facts

  • Makes up 90% of all atoms in the universe
  • Name means "water-former" in Greek
  • Fuel for stars through nuclear fusion
  • Used in Space Shuttle main engines
  • Has three isotopes: Protium, Deuterium, Tritium
  • Promising clean energy source for the future

Atomic Structure

1 proton, 0 neutrons, 1 electron (Protium isotope)
