Carbon (C)

Atomic Number: 6

Basic Information

Symbol: C
Atomic Mass: 12.011 u
Category: Nonmetal
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[He] 2s² 2p²

Key Properties

  • Forms diamonds (hardest natural material)
  • Forms graphite (soft, conductive)
  • Basis of organic chemistry
  • Sublimes at 3652°C (does not melt)
  • Fourth most abundant element in the universe

Discovery & History

Known since ancient times (charcoal, soot). Recognized as an element by Antoine Lavoisier in 1789.

Name comes from Latin "carbo" meaning charcoal.

Historical Timeline:
  • Ancient: Charcoal used for metal smelting
  • 1789: Lavoisier lists carbon as an element
  • 1796: Smithson Tennant proves diamond is pure carbon
  • 1985: Fullerenes (C60) discovered

Common Uses

Diamond jewelry & cutting tools
Pencil "lead" (graphite)
Fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas)
Laboratory electrodes
Water purification (activated carbon)
Steel production
Carbon fiber composites
Basis of all life (organic chemistry)

Interesting Facts

  • Diamonds and graphite are both pure carbon – they differ only in crystal structure
  • Carbon has the highest melting/sublimation point of all elements (3652°C)
  • Graphene – a single layer of graphite – is 200 times stronger than steel
  • Carbon-14 dating can determine the age of organic materials up to 60,000 years
  • Carbon is the fourth most abundant element in the universe
  • Fullerenes (buckyballs) are spherical carbon molecules used in nanotechnology

Atomic Structure

6 protons, 6 neutrons, 6 electrons
