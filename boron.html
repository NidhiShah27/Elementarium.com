Boron (B)

Atomic Number: 5

Basic Information

Symbol: B
Atomic Mass: 10.81 u
Category: Metalloid
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
1s² 2s² 2p¹

Key Properties

  • Hard, brittle metalloid
  • High melting point (2076°C)
  • Excellent thermal and electrical insulator
  • Diamagnetic (repelled by magnetic fields)
  • Forms unique cage-like structures

Discovery & History

First isolated by Sir Humphry Davy, Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, and Louis Jacques Thénard in 1808.

Found in minerals like borax, kernite, and ulexite.

Historical Timeline:
  • 1808: First isolated as element
  • 1892: Henri Moissan produces pure boron
  • 1909: Industrial production begins
  • 1950s: Boron fibers developed for composites

Common Uses

Borosilicate glass
Laboratory glassware
Nuclear control rods
Detergents and soaps
Plant micronutrient
Ceramics and enamels
Flame retardants
Semiconductors

Interesting Facts

  • Borosilicate glass (Pyrex) resists thermal shock
  • Borax deposits found in Death Valley, USA
  • Forms unique icosahedral (20-sided) structures
  • Used in nuclear reactors to absorb neutrons
  • Essential micronutrient for plant growth
  • Second hardest element after diamond (boron nitride)

Atomic Structure

5 protons, 6 neutrons, 5 electrons
