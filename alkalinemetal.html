Alkaline Earth Metals

Group 2 Elements

What are Alkaline Earth Metals?

Group: 2 (IIA)
Valence Electrons: 2 (ns² configuration)
Reactivity: Reactive - but less than alkali metals
Common Characteristics:
  • Silvery-white, lustrous metals
  • Harder and denser than alkali metals
  • Higher melting points than Group 1
  • Form +2 cations (lose both valence electrons)
  • Form alkaline hydroxides (hence the name)
  • Found abundantly in Earth's crust

The name "alkaline earth" comes from the Arabic "al-qali" (the ashes) and refers to their basic (alkaline) oxides that were once called "earths."

Key Properties

  • Reactivity Trend: Increases down the group (Be → Mg → Ca → Sr → Ba → Ra)
  • Melting Points: Higher than Group 1, decrease down group
  • Density: Increases down the group
  • Ionization Energy: Second lowest of all elements - easily lose two electrons
  • Water Reaction: Produce hydrogen gas and metal hydroxide (alkaline solution)
  • Flame Tests: Produce characteristic colors (except Be and Mg)
Flame Test Colors: Each alkaline earth metal (except Be and Mg) produces a characteristic flame color:
  • ❤️ Calcium - Brick Red
  • 🔥 Strontium - Bright Red
  • 💚 Barium - Apple Green
  • 🌸 Radium - Crimson Red
  • Beryllium and Magnesium - No characteristic color

Interesting Facts

  • Magnesium is the central atom in chlorophyll - without it, plants couldn't photosynthesize
  • The average human contains about 1kg of calcium - mostly in bones and teeth
  • Strontium and barium salts create red and green fireworks
  • Radium was used in glow-in-the-dark watch faces until its dangers were known
  • Beryllium is found in emeralds and aquamarines (as beryl)
  • Moon rocks contain significant amounts of calcium and magnesium

Reaction with Water

Alkaline earth metals react with water, but less vigorously than alkali metals. Reactivity increases down the group:

Be H₂O
Beryllium + Water

No reaction at room temperature (oxide layer protects it)

Mg H₂O
Magnesium + Water

Very slow with cold water, reacts with steam

Ca H₂O
Calcium + Water

Moderate reaction, bubbles of hydrogen form

Sr H₂O
Strontium + Water

Vigorous reaction, strontium hydroxide forms

Ba H₂O
Barium + Water

Very vigorous reaction, similar to sodium

Safety Note: While calcium and magnesium are essential nutrients, beryllium compounds are highly toxic, and radium is radioactive. Always handle with proper safety protocols.
