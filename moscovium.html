Moscovium (Mc)

Atomic Number: 115

Basic Information

Symbol: Mc
Atomic Mass: [290] u (most stable isotope)
Category: Post-Transition Metal (predicted)
State at Room Temperature: Solid (predicted)
Electron Configuration:
[Rn] 5f¹⁴ 6d¹⁰ 7s² 7p³ (predicted)

Key Properties

  • Synthetic, highly radioactive
  • Named after Moscow, Russia
  • Predicted to be a volatile metal
  • Very short half-life (milliseconds to seconds)
  • Only a few atoms ever created

Discovery & History

Discovered in 2003 by a joint team at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research in Dubna, Russia, and Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, USA. They bombarded americium-243 with calcium-48 ions.

Named after the Moscow region (Moskovskaya Oblast), where the JINR is located.

Historical Timeline:
  • 2003: First atoms produced at Dubna
  • 2015: Confirmed by IUPAC
  • 2016: Officially named moscovium
  • 21st c.: Only a few dozen atoms ever created

Interesting Facts

  • Moscovium is part of the "island of stability" – a predicted region of relatively long-lived superheavy elements
  • Moscovium-290 has a half-life of about 0.8 seconds – unusually long for a superheavy element
  • Relativistic effects may make moscovium behave differently from its lighter homolog, bismuth
  • Some predictions suggest moscovium could be a volatile metal with a low boiling point
  • The element is named after the Moscow region – home to the famous Dubna laboratory where many superheavy elements were discovered
  • Only about 100 atoms of moscovium have ever been made – all have decayed in less than a second

Atomic Structure

115 protons, 175 neutrons (in 290Mc), 115 electrons
