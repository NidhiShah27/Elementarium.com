Alkali Metals

Group 1 Elements

Group: 1 (IA)

Category: Alkali Metal

Elements: Lithium (Li), Sodium (Na), Potassium (K), Rubidium (Rb), Cesium (Cs), Francium (Fr)

General Electron Configuration: ns1

Alkali metals are soft, shiny, highly reactive metals that are never found in their pure form in nature due to their extreme reactivity.

They are stored under oil or in inert atmospheres to prevent reaction with air or moisture.

Key Properties:
  • All have one valence electron which they readily lose to form +1 cations.
  • Reactivity increases down the group (Francium is the most reactive).
  • They have low melting and boiling points compared to other metals.
  • They are excellent conductors of heat and electricity.
  • They produce characteristic flame colors: Li (crimson red), Na (yellow), K (lilac), Rb (red-violet), Cs (blue).
  • Violently react with water to form alkaline hydroxides and hydrogen gas.
  • Essential for many biological processes (e.g., Sodium and Potassium ions for nerve signaling).
Common Uses:
  • Lithium: Rechargeable batteries, mood-stabilizing drugs, lightweight alloys.
  • Sodium: Table salt (NaCl), street lighting, coolant in nuclear reactors.
  • Potassium: Fertilizers, soaps, electrolyte in cells.
  • Cesium: Atomic clocks, photoelectric cells.

Alkali Metals (Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs) stored under oil.

Reaction with Water

When dropped in water, alkali metals fizz, move rapidly, and can ignite the hydrogen gas produced.

2M(s) + 2H2O(l) → 2MOH(aq) + H2(g) + heat
