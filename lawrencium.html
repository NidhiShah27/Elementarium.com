Lawrencium (Lr)

Atomic Number: 103

Basic Information

Symbol: Lr
Atomic Mass: [266] u (most stable isotope)
Category: Actinide
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Rn] 5f¹⁴ 7s² 6d¹ (predicted)

Key Properties

  • Highly radioactive synthetic metal, last member of the actinide series
  • Named after Ernest O. Lawrence, inventor of the cyclotron
  • Completes the 5f electron series — final actinide element
  • Predicted to have a stable +3 oxidation state like other late actinides
  • Only produced in atom quantities; chemical studies extremely challenging

Discovery & History

First synthesized in 1961 by Albert Ghiorso, Torbjørn Sikkeland, Almon Larsh, and Robert M. Latimer at the University of California, Berkeley. They bombarded californium-252 with boron-10 and boron-11 ions in the Heavy Ion Linear Accelerator (HILAC), producing lawrencium-258 (half-life ~4 seconds).

Named after Ernest O. Lawrence, founder of the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory and inventor of the cyclotron. The discovery was confirmed by Soviet scientists at Dubna in 1965.

Common Uses

Basic research in nuclear physics
Study of relativistic effects in heavy elements
Investigation of the end of the actinide series
No commercial applications (extremely rare)
Calibration of alpha and fission detectors
Study of spontaneous fission properties
Synthesis of rutherfordium (element 104)
Tracer experiments with single atoms

Interesting Facts

  • Lawrencium is the last member of the actinide series — element 103 closes the 5f electron shell.
  • Its most stable isotope, ²⁶⁶Lr, has a half-life of about 11 hours — the longest-lived of any superheavy element beyond lawrencium.
  • The first chemical studies in 1970 used only 35 atoms of lawrencium to determine its elution behavior on cation exchange columns.
  • Relativistic effects significantly influence its electronic structure, making its chemistry deviate slightly from simple predictions.
  • Lawrencium is named after Ernest Lawrence, whose cyclotron technology made the discovery of transuranium elements possible.
  • It is produced by bombarding berkelium or californium targets with oxygen or neon ions in heavy-ion accelerators.

Atomic Structure

103 protons, 163 neutrons (in 266Lr), 103 electrons

Try These Lawrencium Reactions

Lawrencium chemistry is dominated by the +3 oxidation state, similar to its lanthanide homolog lutetium. The +1 and +2 states are not stable in aqueous solution. Lr³⁺ behaves like a typical trivalent actinide but with relativistic effects influencing its ionic radius.

Experiment Idea: Lawrencium's +3 oxidation state can be studied by cation exchange chromatography, where it elutes similarly to other trivalent actinides. Complexation studies with alpha-hydroxyisobutyrate (α-HIB) show Lr³⁺ behavior consistent with its position as the heaviest actinide. Due to the extremely short half-lives and atom quantities, automated rapid chemistry systems (like the SISAK or ARCA) are essential for all experiments.
