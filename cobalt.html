Cobalt (Co)

Atomic Number: 27

Basic Information

Symbol: Co
Atomic Mass: 58.933194 u
Category: Transition Metal (Group 9)
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Ar] 3d⁷ 4s² [citation:1][citation:2]

Key Properties

  • Hard, lustrous, bluish-gray metal [citation:1]
  • Melting point: 1495°C, Boiling point: 2927°C [citation:5]
  • Oxidation-resistant and stable in air [citation:3]
  • Ferromagnetic (retains magnetism at high temperatures) [citation:7]
  • Imparts brilliant blue color to glass and ceramics [citation:2]

Discovery & History

Discovered in 1735 by Georg Brandt in Sweden. He was the first person to discover a metal unknown in ancient times. The name comes from the German word "kobold" meaning goblin or evil spirit [citation:4][citation:8].

Miners gave it this name because cobalt-containing ores were difficult to smelt and released toxic arsenic fumes, which they blamed on mischievous goblins [citation:1].

Historical Timeline:
  • Ancient: Cobalt compounds used to color glass (Egypt, Persia) [citation:4]
  • 1735: Brandt isolates and identifies cobalt as a new element [citation:1]
  • 1780: Cobalt becomes popular for coloring porcelain [citation:3]
  • 20th c.: Vital for jet engines and rechargeable batteries [citation:8]

Common Uses

Lithium-ion batteries [citation:3]
Jet engine superalloys [citation:2]
Blue pigments (cobalt blue) [citation:5]
Hard metals (carbide tools) [citation:7]
Magnetic recording media [citation:1]
Radiation therapy (Co-60) [citation:4]
Electroplating (jewelry) [citation:8]
Catalysts (petroleum) [citation:6]

Interesting Facts

  • "Cobalt blue" is a famous, stable pigment used by artists like Van Gogh and Vermeer. It's made by mixing cobalt oxide with alumina – the resulting blue is both intense and permanent [citation:4].
  • Cobalt-60 is a radioactive isotope used to treat cancer (gamma rays). It's produced by bombarding cobalt-59 with neutrons in nuclear reactors and is a mainstay of radiation therapy [citation:2].
  • Cobalt is essential for modern electric vehicle batteries – most lithium-ion batteries use lithium cobalt oxide (LiCoO₂) as the cathode material [citation:3].
  • Cobalt is essential for ruminant animals (sheep, cattle) as part of vitamin B12. The bacteria in their stomachs use cobalt to synthesize this vitamin, which is why cobalt salts are added to animal feed [citation:5].
  • Vitamin B12 (cobalamin) is the only vitamin that contains a metal atom (cobalt). It's crucial for nerve function and blood cell formation in humans [citation:7].
  • Ancient Persians created cobalt-blue glass around 2000 BCE. Cobalt-colored glass has been found in Egyptian tombs and Mesopotamian artifacts, prized for its rich, stable color [citation:8].

Atomic Structure

27 protons, 32 neutrons (in 59Co), 27 electrons

Try These Cobalt Reactions

Cobalt commonly exhibits +2 and +3 oxidation states, forming colorful compounds, particularly deep blue complexes. The color changes are sensitive to hydration and coordination environment [citation:1]. Here are some combinations to explore:

Co S

Co N

Co Cr
Co + Chromium (stellite alloys)
Experiment Idea (Conceptual): Cobalt chloride (CoCl₂) is a humidity indicator – it changes color from blue (dry, anhydrous) to pink (hydrated, CoCl₂·6H₂O). This reversible reaction is used in desiccant silica gel to show when it's saturated with moisture. The color change occurs because the coordination environment around Co²⁺ changes from tetrahedral (blue) to octahedral (pink) – a beautiful demonstration of coordination chemistry and the effect of hydration on color [citation:1].
