Lithium (Li)

Atomic Number: 3

Basic Information

Symbol: Li
Atomic Mass: 6.941 u
Category: Alkali Metal
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
1s² 2s¹

Key Properties

  • Lightest metal on the periodic table
  • Highly reactive with water and air
  • Low density (floats on oil)
  • Excellent electrical conductor
  • Lowest melting point of all metals (180.5°C)

Discovery & History

Discovered by Johan August Arfwedson in 1817 in Sweden.

Found in minerals like spodumene, lepidolite, and petalite.

Historical Timeline:
  • 1817: Discovered in petalite mineral
  • 1818: First isolated by William Thomas Brande
  • 1923: Commercial production begins
  • 1970s: Lithium-ion battery development starts

Common Uses

Rechargeable batteries
Psychiatric medication
Industrial lubricants
Glass and ceramics
Electric vehicles
Air conditioning
Aerospace alloys
Mobile devices

Interesting Facts

  • Only metal that floats on oil (lowest density)
  • Used as mood stabilizer for bipolar disorder
  • Powers most modern rechargeable devices
  • Reacts violently with water, producing hydrogen gas
  • Found in stars and formed during Big Bang nucleosynthesis
  • Key to green energy transition through batteries

Atomic Structure

3 protons, 4 neutrons, 3 electrons
