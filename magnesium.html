Magnesium (Mg)

Atomic Number: 12

Basic Information

Symbol: Mg
Atomic Mass: 24.305 u
Category: Alkaline Earth Metal
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Ne] 3s²

Key Properties

  • Very lightweight (1/4 the density of steel)
  • Burns with brilliant white flame
  • Forms protective oxide layer
  • Good electrical conductor
  • Eighth most abundant element in Earth's crust

Discovery & History

Recognized as an element by Joseph Black in 1755. First isolated by Humphry Davy in 1808 through electrolysis of magnesia and mercury oxide.

The name comes from Magnesia, a district in Greece where magnesium compounds were found.

Historical Timeline:
  • 1755: Black identifies magnesium as an element
  • 1808: Davy isolates pure magnesium
  • 1886: Commercial production begins (electrolysis)
  • 20th c.: Vital for aerospace and automotive

Common Uses

Aerospace alloys (lightweight)
Automotive parts (wheels, engines)
Electronics (laptop cases)
Camera bodies
Fireworks and flares
Antacids (milk of magnesia)
Chlorophyll (essential for plants)
Sacrificial anodes (corrosion protection)

Interesting Facts

  • Magnesium is at the center of every chlorophyll molecule – without it, photosynthesis couldn't happen
  • Magnesium burns so brightly it was used in early flashbulbs for photography
  • It's the lightest structural metal – 1/3 lighter than aluminum, yet strong enough for aircraft
  • Seawater contains vast amounts of magnesium – about 1.3 grams per liter
  • Magnesium ions are essential for over 300 enzyme reactions in the human body
  • When magnesium burns, it produces ultraviolet light as well as visible – never look directly at it!

Atomic Structure

12 protons, 12 neutrons, 12 electrons
