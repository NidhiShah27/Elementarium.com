Erbium (Er)

Atomic Number: 68

Basic Information

Symbol: Er
Atomic Mass: 167.259 u
Category: Lanthanide
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Xe] 4f¹² 6s²

Key Properties

  • Soft, silvery-metal, relatively stable in air
  • Pink salts and oxides
  • Named after the village Ytterby, Sweden
  • 6 naturally occurring isotopes
  • Slowly oxidizes, reacts with water and acids

Discovery & History

Discovered in 1843 by Swedish chemist Carl Gustaf Mosander. He separated "yttria" into three fractions: yttria, erbia, and terbia. The pink fraction he named erbia.

Named after Ytterby, the Swedish village where the mineral gadolinite was found – the same source for yttrium, terbium, and ytterbium.

Historical Timeline:
  • 1843: Mosander isolates erbium
  • 1878: Jean Charles Galissard de Marignac identifies it as distinct
  • 1905: Georges Urbain purifies erbium
  • Modern: Essential for fiber optics and medical lasers

Common Uses

Fiber optic amplifiers (Er-doped fiber)
Er:YAG lasers (skin resurfacing, dentistry)
Pink glass and crystal coloring
Infrared-absorbing safety glasses
Nuclear reactor control rods
Metallurgy additive (strengthens alloys)
Phosphor in lighting and displays
Microwave ferrites

Interesting Facts

  • Erbium is the workhorse of fiber optic communications – erbium-doped fiber amplifiers (EDFAs) boost light signals directly without converting them to electricity.
  • Er:YAG lasers (2940 nm) are perfectly tuned to water absorption, making them ideal for precise skin resurfacing and dental procedures with minimal heat damage.
  • Erbium is one of four elements named after Ytterby (with yttrium, terbium, ytterbium) – the small Swedish quarry that has more elements named after it than any other place.
  • Pink erbium oxide (Er₂O₃) is used to color glass and cubic zirconia, producing delicate pink hues prized in jewelry.
  • It has a high neutron absorption cross-section, making it useful in nuclear reactor control rods.
  • Abundance in Earth's crust is about 3.5 ppm, similar to silver.

Atomic Structure

68 protons, 99 neutrons (in 167Er), 68 electrons
