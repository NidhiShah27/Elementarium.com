Arsenic (As)

Atomic Number: 33

Basic Information

Symbol: As
Atomic Mass: 74.9216 u
Category: Metalloid
State at Room Temperature: Solid
Electron Configuration:
[Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s² 4p³

Key Properties

  • Highly toxic in many compounds
  • Exists in gray, yellow, and black allotropes
  • Semiconducting properties
  • Sublimes at 615°C (skips liquid phase)
  • Can be found in nature as a pure element

Discovery & History

Known since ancient times – arsenic compounds were used by alchemists. Albertus Magnus is credited with isolating the element around 1250.

The name comes from the Greek "arsenikon" meaning potent, referring to its powerful properties.

Historical Timeline:
  • ~1250: Albertus Magnus isolates arsenic
  • 1649: Schröder publishes preparation methods
  • 19th c.: Used in pigments and rat poisons
  • 20th c.: Important semiconductor dopant

Common Uses

Semiconductor doping (GaAs)
Pesticides and herbicides
Wood preservatives
Glass manufacturing
Arsenic trioxide (cancer treatment)
Gallium arsenide (LEDs, solar cells)
Alloys (improves hardness)
Rat poisons (historical)

Interesting Facts

  • Arsenic is famously known as the "poison of kings" – it was used for murder because symptoms resemble cholera
  • Napoleon may have been poisoned by arsenic – traces found in his hair
  • Gallium arsenide (GaAs) is faster than silicon – used in high-frequency electronics
  • Some bacteria use arsenic instead of phosphorus in their DNA (controversial discovery)
  • Pure arsenic is relatively safe to handle (has a metallic luster), but its compounds are deadly
  • Realgar and orpiment are arsenic sulfide minerals used as pigments since ancient times

Atomic Structure

33 protons, 42 neutrons, 33 electrons
