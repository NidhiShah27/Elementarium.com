Alkali Metals

Group 1 Elements (Except Hydrogen)

What are Alkali Metals?

Group: 1 (IA)
Valence Electrons: 1 (ns¹ configuration)
Reactivity: Extremely reactive - never found in pure form in nature
Common Characteristics:
  • Soft metals (can be cut with a knife)
  • Low density and melting points
  • Shiny when freshly cut (tarnish quickly in air)
  • Excellent conductors of heat and electricity
  • Form strong alkaline hydroxides when reacted with water

Alkali metals are so named because they form alkaline (basic) solutions when they react with water.

Key Properties

  • Reactivity Trend: Increases down the group (Li → Na → K → Rb → Cs → Fr)
  • Melting Points: Decrease down the group
  • Density: Increases down the group (except potassium)
  • Ionization Energy: Lowest of all elements - easily lose their single valence electron
  • Water Reaction: Produce hydrogen gas and metal hydroxide (alkaline solution)
Flame Test Colors: Each alkali metal produces a characteristic flame color when heated:
  • ❤️ Lithium - Crimson Red
  • 🔥 Sodium - Yellow/Orange
  • 💜 Potassium - Lilac
  • 🌸 Rubidium - Red-Violet
  • 💙 Cesium - Blue

Interesting Facts

  • Cesium is so reactive it explodes instantly when dropped in water
  • Sodium and potassium are soft enough to cut with a butter knife
  • Cesium atomic clocks are accurate to within one second in 100 million years
  • Francium is so rare, scientists have never isolated a visible amount
  • Potassium is essential for nerve function and heartbeat regulation
  • Lithium was one of the three elements created in the Big Bang

Reaction with Water

All alkali metals react vigorously with water, producing hydrogen gas and a metal hydroxide solution. The reactivity increases dramatically down the group:

Li H₂O
Lithium + Water

Fizzes steadily, floats on water

Na H₂O
Sodium + Water

Melts into a ball, darts around surface

K H₂O
Potassium + Water

Lilac flame, ignites hydrogen gas

Rb H₂O
Rubidium + Water

Explosive reaction, ignites immediately

Cs H₂O
Cesium + Water

Violent explosion on contact

Safety Warning: Alkali metals should never be handled without proper training and equipment. They react violently with moisture and can cause severe burns or explosions!
